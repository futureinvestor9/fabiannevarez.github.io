CRM operations — live app

DTD — CRM Cadence Planner

DTD4X (“Do The Database”) is the quarterly follow-up system I designed and ran to keep a ~400-lead real-estate pipeline from leaking value — four deliberate touches per contact each quarter instead of memory-based, ad-hoc follow-up. I originally operated it in Follow Up Boss with stages, tags, and Smart Lists; this interactive app rebuilds the system in the browser so you can see exactly how the cadence works. (Built with AI assistance, like the rest of this portfolio.)

Open the live app →

Runs in your browser. Data is stored locally on your device — nothing is sent anywhere.

The problem it solves

A book of ~400 relationships is only an asset if you actually stay in touch with it. Without a system, follow-up happens to whoever you remember — the pipeline quietly leaks. DTD4X makes the cadence explicit: four touches per contact per quarter — across call, text, email, and a value-add touch — so every relationship is worked deliberately, sixteen times a year instead of at random. This app demonstrates that rhythm with a four-touch rotation (call, text, note card, social) that schedules every contact for all four types across a 13-week quarter.

What it does

Quarterly rotation

Spreads the four touch types across a 13-week quarter so the workload is even, not bunched.

Contacts, stages & tags

A structured record for every contact — stage in the relationship, tags, and touch history.

Touchpoint tracking

Call / text / note-card / social, checked off per contact, with weekly and quarterly progress.

CSV import & export

Bring an existing contact list in, and take the data back out — no lock-in, portable records.

What it demonstrates

  • CRM operations & pipeline discipline. A concrete follow-up system with measurable coverage — the core of a revenue-operations cadence.
  • Data modeling. Contacts, stages, tags, and touchpoints modeled as clean, relatable records with import/export in and out of CSV.
  • Process design. A repeatable rule (16 touches/contact/year) turned into a tool anyone can run the same way — not a one-off spreadsheet.
  • Built and shipped. DTD4X is a cadence system I actually designed and operated; this app makes it tangible and interactive (built with AI assistance), not just described.
