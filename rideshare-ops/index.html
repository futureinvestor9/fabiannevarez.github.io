How the system came together

Phase 1 · Fall 2025 — the grind

High volume, gut-feel decisions

198 hours online in a single month for $4,835.44 — $24.42/hr across 2,671 miles. Accept/reject calls were instinct, and every marginal trip added miles the car absorbed as fuel, maintenance, and depreciation.

Phase 2 · April 2026 — the build

Baseline, thresholds, tooling

Logged and analyzed 75+ trips; validated a $32.96/hr gross baseline. Used AI tools to draft accept/reject thresholds, then cross-checked every recommendation against actual earnings before adopting it. Added Mystro + Gridwise (Apr 15) to run both platforms as one operation.

Phase 3 · Apr–Jul 2026 — operating

Rules, not vibes

Every incoming trip scored against a three-tier filter on $/mile and $/hour, run across Uber and Lyft at once. Combined monthly take-home climbed from ~$1,084 to ~$7,700 as the rules took hold.

The decision framework: a three-tier trip filter

Every trip offer is scored on two rates before acceptance. Rules were drafted with AI assistance, then validated against 75+ actual trips and real earnings before adoption.

The middle band is evaluated by context — direction, time of day, surge, and repositioning value — not accepted by default.

Preserving the asset

A car is the operation's capital equipment. Every mile carries fuel, maintenance, and depreciation, so the filter optimizes revenue per mile — not just per hour.

Methodology & sources

  1. Lyft payouts: 73 dated Lyft Direct (Payfare) transfer records, September 2025 – July 2026, aggregated by month. These are take-home deposit transfers, not gross fares; timing of a transfer can lag the rides that earned it.
  2. Uber earnings & mileage (primary): Uber's official Tax Summaries (Mar–Jun 2026) and 2025 1099-K. Per month: gross earnings, online miles, and completed trips — e.g. May 2026: 481 trips, 5,123 miles, $13,235.22 gross. Dollars-per-mile is computed directly from these official figures ($1.45 → $2.03 → $2.58 → $2.82). 2025 gross per the 1099-K was $24,024.49 across 1,302 trips.
  3. Pre-system benchmark: Uber's monthly driver recap (fall 2025): $4,835.44 over 198 online hours ($24.42/hr) — used only for the hourly benchmark, since the tax summaries report miles and trips but not online hours.
  4. Validated baseline: personal trip log of 75+ trips — $1,946.53 across 59.1 tracked online hours ($32.96/hr gross), built in Excel/Google Sheets with Mystro and Gridwise operating data.
  5. Honest comparison note: the before and after hourly figures come from different sources (platform recap vs. tracked trip log). Both are stated only to their verified endpoints, and neither is extrapolated.
  6. Ride volume & take-home rate: Lyft's weekly performance emails (completed-ride counts) and its May earnings breakdown ($215.01 passenger payments → $115.80 driver earnings, a 54% take-home rate after Lyft's 26% fee and 20% insurance/taxes/fees).
  7. Cross-source check: Lyft's stated May net ($115.80) and the May total from the independent payout records ($122.34) agree within 5.6% — two records that don't reference each other, pointing to the same number.
  8. What isn't shown, and why: Uber, Lyft, and Mystro don't export individual trip logs, so per-trip scatter and accept/decline counts can't be reproduced here — the three-tier filter is shown as its documented rules. The monthly $/mile figures, however, are real and official (Uber Tax Summaries), not estimated.
  9. AI-assisted, human-validated: Claude and ChatGPT were used to structure the analysis and draft threshold candidates; no rule was adopted until it was cross-checked against actual earnings outcomes.
